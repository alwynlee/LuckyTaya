Commission Structures
📊
Revenue Share
RevShare · Recurring
Affiliate earns a percentage of the net gaming revenue (NGR) generated by players they refer — for the lifetime of the player's activity on the platform. The most common long-term affiliate model.
L1 NGR = Deposits − Withdrawals − Bonuses − Platform Costs
L1 Commission = L1 NGR × L1 RevShare Rate
L2 Override = L1 Commission × L2 Sub-Aff Rate
💰
Cost Per Acquisition
CPA · One-time
Affiliate earns a fixed flat fee for each new player that registers, completes KYC, and makes a qualifying first deposit above the minimum threshold. Payment is made once per qualifying player.
Qualifying Trigger = KYC Complete + FTD ≥ ₱500
CPA Payment = Fixed rate per qualified player
L2 CPA Override = Fixed sub-override per qualified player
⚖️
Hybrid
RevShare + CPA · Combined
A negotiated combination of CPA (paid on qualifying first deposit) plus a reduced RevShare rate on ongoing NGR. Preferred by high-volume partners who want immediate income and long-term upside.
Hybrid CPA = Reduced fixed rate per FTD
Hybrid RevShare = Reduced % NGR (typically 50–70% of standard rate)
Must be individually negotiated and documented

⚖️
Margin Health — Total Override Cap Principle The sum of all RevShare rates and override percentages across the full 5-level chain for any single player's NGR must not exceed 65% of that NGR. The Affiliate Manager is responsible for verifying this ceiling is not breached when approving new sub-affiliate agreements or rate negotiations. If a proposed structure would push total payouts above this threshold, rates at the lower levels must be reduced before the agreement is signed.
Example Cascade Calculation
Worked Example — ₱100,000 NGR from a single L5-referred player
Assumes a full 5-level chain is active. Each upstream level earns an override on the level directly below them. Total payouts cascade upward simultaneously — they are not deducted sequentially from the player's NGR.
Level Partner Role Basis Rate Calculation Earnings
L5 Referring partner (direct referral) Player NGR 10% RevShare ₱100,000 × 10% ₱10,000
L4 L5's recruiter L5 Earnings 2% override on L5 ₱10,000 × 2% ₱200
L3 L4's recruiter L4 + L5 Earnings 3% override on downstream (₱10,000 + ₱200) × 3% ₱306
L2 L3's recruiter L3 + L4 + L5 Earnings 4% override on downstream (₱10,000 + ₱200 + ₱306) × 4% ₱420
L1 L2's recruiter L2 + L3 + L4 + L5 Earnings 5% override on downstream (₱10,000 + ₱200 + ₱306 + ₱420) × 5% ₱546
Total payout on ₱100,000 NGR (all levels combined) ₱11,472
= 11.47% of NGR
Note on override calculation method: Two methods exist — flat NGR override (all levels calculate their override as a % of the player's original NGR) or cascading earnings override (each level earns a % of the level directly below). The example above uses cascading earnings. The flat NGR method results in higher upstream payouts. The chosen method must be explicitly documented in every affiliate agreement to avoid disputes. The Affiliate Manager selects the method at programme setup.

Application Actions
  • Affiliate completes the online application form: company name or individual name, website URL or social/digital channel, traffic sources (SEO, paid, social, influencer, community), target audience, estimated monthly player referral volume, and preferred commission structure
  • Automated pre-screen runs immediately: check website URL against blacklisted domain list, verify the traffic source category is permitted under PAGCOR affiliate marketing guidelines, confirm the applicant is not on the AMLC blacklist or sanctions list

KYC Requirements
  • Individual affiliate: Government-issued ID (Philippine passport, driver's licence, UMID, SSS), proof of address (utility bill or bank statement within 90 days), selfie with ID, TIN (Tax Identification Number) from BIR
  • Corporate affiliate: SEC registration certificate, BIR Certificate of Registration (COR), Articles of Incorporation, ID of authorised signatory, proof of business address, and bank details for commission payments
  • KYC Analyst runs PEP and sanctions screening on all individual signatories and beneficial owners. Any PEP match requires KYC Senior review before the affiliate agreement can be activated
  • Verify that the affiliate's payment account (GCash, Maya, or bank) is registered in the same name as the KYC identity documents — mismatches must be resolved before commissions are ever paid
  • For corporate affiliates with majority foreign ownership: confirm whether a PAGCOR Special Affiliate Licence is required under current regulations
🇵🇭 BIR Tax Obligations: Affiliate commission payments in the Philippines are subject to withholding tax. Individual affiliates: 10% CWT (Creditable Withholding Tax) on commissions if registered professional, or 8% if under the 8% income tax option. Corporate affiliates: 2% EWT (Expanded Withholding Tax). The operator is responsible for withholding and remitting the correct tax to BIR. All affiliates must provide a valid TIN before the first commission payment is processed.

Negotiation Process
  • Affiliate Manager presents the standard rate card based on the affiliate's estimated monthly player volume and traffic quality tier
  • Determine the commission structure: RevShare, CPA, or Hybrid. Hybrid structures require Head of Affiliates approval before presentation to the partner
  • Assign the affiliate's starting level (L1 for all new direct partners). If the affiliate is being recruited by an existing L1/L2 partner as a sub-affiliate, assign the appropriate sub-level and ensure the total override chain does not breach the 65% margin cap
  • Agree the negative carryover policy: whether a monthly negative NGR balance (operator loss from a player who wins more than they deposit) is carried forward and deducted from future earnings, or reset to zero each month. Default policy: negative carryover applies. This must be explicitly stated in the contract
  • Agree the cookie window: the period during which a player referral is attributed to the affiliate after clicking a tracking link. Standard: 30 days last-click attribution. Premium partners: up to 90 days negotiable
  • Affiliate Manager submits finalised terms to Legal for contract generation. Standard template used for L1–L3. Custom contract required for L4–L5 (approved by Head of Affiliates)
Key Contract Terms to Confirm
  • Commission structure type and rate (RevShare %, CPA amount, or Hybrid combination)
  • Level assignment (L1 direct or L2–L5 sub-affiliate under named upline partner)
  • Sub-affiliate recruitment rights — which downstream levels the partner is permitted to recruit
  • Negative carryover policy: applies / does not apply. If applies: cap on maximum monthly carry (e.g. maximum 3 months carry before reset)
  • Cookie window duration and attribution method (last-click)
  • Minimum monthly payment threshold: commissions below ₱500 rolled to next month
  • Payment method and schedule (monthly, within 15 days of month-end)
  • Prohibited marketing practices per PAGCOR guidelines
  • BIR withholding tax rate and operator's responsibility to remit
  • Termination, clawback, and fraud provisions

Portal Setup Actions
  • Create the affiliate's portal account within 24 hours of contract signing. Set commission structure, level, and rate configuration exactly as per the signed contract

Player Attribution & Commission Accrual
First click tracking · FTD qualification · RevShare accrual · CPA trigger logic
ONGOING · MONTHLY
▾
Attribution Logic
  • Cookie tracking: when a player clicks the affiliate's tracking link, a cookie is stored in their browser. If the player registers within the cookie window (30 or 90 days), the registration is attributed to that affiliate
  • Last-click attribution: if a player clicks links from multiple affiliates, the last affiliate's link before registration takes credit. This is the standard model — first-click attribution may be negotiated for specific high-value partners on request
  • FTD qualification for CPA: a player qualifies for a CPA payment only when: (a) KYC is fully approved, (b) the player makes a first deposit of at least ₱500, and (c) the deposit is not reversed or charged back within 30 days
  • RevShare accrual: begins from the calendar month in which the player makes their first deposit. NGR is calculated monthly. Commission accrues continuously for as long as the player is active and the affiliate agreement is in force
  • Negative NGR: if a player's monthly NGR is negative (operator pays out more than it receives), this negative amount is applied to the affiliate's account per the agreed negative carryover policy
Multi-Level Attribution Rules
  • A referred player is permanently attributed to the affiliate who generated the tracking link click that led to their registration — this attribution does not change even if the affiliate's level or rate changes later
  • Override commissions cascade upward simultaneously in the same monthly processing run — upstream partners do not need to wait for downstream commissions to be paid first
  • If an affiliate is terminated, their direct player attributions remain active. Players continue to generate RevShare that is held by the operator. Pending commissions at termination are paid per the termination clause in the contract
  • If a player was referred by an affiliate who was later found to have engaged in fraud (fake traffic, self-referral), all commissions generated by that player referral are subject to clawback regardless of how far upstream the override chain goes
🇵🇭 GCash / Maya Attribution Note: Some PH players use GCash to register and deposit without clicking a tracked link (direct URL entry). Ensure the affiliate platform supports server-side tracking in addition to browser cookies, particularly for mobile-first players who use GCash's in-app browser, which may block third-party cookies. This is a common attribution gap in the PH market.

Fraud Detection — Affiliate-Specific Patterns
FR
Affiliate Fraud Detection & Response
Fake traffic · Self-referral · Bonus stacking · Commission manipulation · Sub-affiliate fraud
ZERO TOLERANCE
▾
Fraud Signals to Monitor
  • Self-referral: affiliate registers a player account using their own device, IP, or payment method and generates commissions on their own deposits. Cross-check all FTD qualifications against the affiliate's own KYC data
  • Fake traffic / incentivised registration: affiliate pays third parties to register and make a small FTD to trigger CPA payouts, with no genuine intention to play. Indicators: very low deposit amounts at exactly the minimum threshold, immediate withdrawal after FTD, high FTD-to-churn rate
  • Bonus abuse through referrals: affiliate sends players who are in coordinated bonus abuse rings. Monitor for high volumes of player referrals from a single affiliate who all claim the same promotion and never return
  • Commission manipulation: affiliate with sub-affiliate recruitment rights creates fictitious sub-affiliates to generate override commissions without any real traffic being sent. Cross-reference sub-affiliate KYC against affiliate's own identity documents
  • Cookie stuffing: affiliate uses technical methods to drop tracking cookies on browsers of users who never saw or clicked an affiliate link — inflating attribution counts without genuine referrals
Response Actions
  • Affiliate Manager flags the suspicious pattern and raises a formal investigation case — do not immediately terminate without evidence
  • Coordinate with the Fraud team (Tier 2 Senior Analyst) to review the player accounts associated with the affiliate's tracking links
  • If fraud is confirmed: withhold the current month's commission payment pending Head of Affiliates review. Do not inform the affiliate at this stage
  • Head of Affiliates reviews evidence and makes the final call: (a) commission clawback and formal warning, (b) immediate termination with full clawback of all fraudulent commissions, or (c) legal action if scale warrants
  • Clawback is applied to all commissions traceable to fraudulent activity — including override commissions paid to upstream levels if they were knowingly complicit. Document all clawback actions in the affiliate's CRM record
⚠
Warning + ClawbackMinor or first-time violation. Commissions from fraudulent referrals reversed. Final warning issued in writing.
⛔
Termination + Full ClawbackSystematic fraud. Agreement terminated. All fraudulent commissions clawed back. Account permanently closed. Platform and tracking links deactivated.
Dispute Resolution Process
Step 1
Affiliate Raises Dispute
Via portal ticket within the 4-day dispute window. Must specify: month, amount disputed, reason, and supporting evidence.
Step 2
Affiliate Manager Reviews
Checks portal reports against the CRM source data within 3 business days. Identifies any system error, tracking failure, or legitimate discrepancy.
Step 3
Resolution & Adjustment
If valid: adjustment approved by Affiliate Manager, applied to next payment run. If rejected: written explanation sent with evidence. Affiliate may escalate to Head of Affiliates within 5 days.
Partner Termination
Voluntary Termination
  • Affiliate provides 30-day written notice via the portal or email to the Affiliate Manager
  • During the 30-day notice period: tracking links remain active, player attributions continue, and RevShare accrues normally
  • Final commission statement issued within 15 days of the termination effective date. All earned and unpaid commissions paid in the final payment run
  • Player attributions for direct referrals are retained by the operator — the terminated affiliate forfeits future RevShare on those players from the termination date
  • If the affiliate had active sub-affiliates: sub-affiliate contracts must be reviewed. Sub-affiliates may be migrated to direct L1 agreements with the operator at the Affiliate Manager's discretion
Involuntary Termination (Breach)
  • Immediate termination: fraud confirmed, PAGCOR marketing violations, KYC document forgery, or material breach of the affiliate agreement
  • Tracking links deactivated immediately. Portal access suspended. No further commission accrual from termination date
  • Pending commissions withheld pending clawback assessment. Any fraudulent commissions already paid are subject to recovery action
  • Head of Affiliates approves all involuntary terminations. Legal team engaged if clawback value exceeds ₱50,000 or if criminal activity is suspected
  • Compliance team notified if termination is related to AML or KYC concerns — may trigger AMLC STR process if warranted

Affiliate Glossary

Key terms, commission mechanics, Philippines regulatory definitions, and payment infrastructure references for the Affiliate Management workflow.

Commission & Programme Terms
RevShare
Revenue Share
A commission model where the affiliate earns a percentage of the net gaming revenue (NGR) generated by their referred players on an ongoing monthly basis. Rates are expressed as a percentage of NGR and vary by affiliate level (L1–L5) and traffic quality tier.
CPA
Cost Per Acquisition
A flat-fee commission paid once per qualifying player — defined as a player who completes KYC and makes a first-time deposit (FTD) above the minimum threshold. CPA is not recurring. If the player never deposits again, no further CPA commission is generated.
NGR
Net Gaming Revenue
The revenue used as the basis for RevShare calculations. Calculated as: player deposits minus player withdrawals minus bonuses paid minus platform operating costs (payment fees, game licensing costs). If NGR is negative in a given month (operator pays out more than it receives), this affects the affiliate's earnings per the negative carryover policy.
FTD
First-Time Deposit
A player's very first deposit after completing KYC registration. The FTD is the trigger event for CPA commission payment. The minimum qualifying FTD amount is defined in the affiliate agreement (standard: ₱500). FTDs that are charged back within 30 days do not qualify for CPA.
Negative Carryover
Monthly Negative Balance Policy
When a player wins significantly in a given month, the affiliate's NGR for that player goes negative. Under a negative carryover policy, this negative balance is carried forward and deducted from future months' earnings before the affiliate receives any commission. Without negative carryover (monthly reset), the affiliate's balance resets to zero each month regardless of negative outcomes.
Cookie Window
Attribution Tracking Period
The period during which a player who clicks an affiliate tracking link will be attributed to that affiliate if they register. Standard: 30 days. Premium partners: up to 90 days. If the cookie expires before the player registers, no attribution is recorded for that affiliate.
Sub-Affiliate
Downstream Network Partner
An affiliate recruited into the network by an existing affiliate partner (their upline). Sub-affiliates earn commissions on their own direct player referrals. Their upline earns an override commission on the sub-affiliate's earnings — this is the mechanism that creates the multi-level structure.
Override
Upstream Commission on Downstream Earnings
The commission earned by an upline affiliate on the earnings of the affiliates they recruited. For example: an L1 partner earns a 5% override on L2's RevShare commissions. Overrides cascade through all active levels simultaneously in the monthly processing run.
Clawback
Commission Reversal
The recovery of commissions already paid when a player's FTD is reversed (chargeback), or when fraud is confirmed in the affiliate's referral activity. Clawback provisions must be explicitly stated in the affiliate agreement and specify the time window within which clawback can be applied (typically up to 6 months).
Last-Click Attribution
Default Attribution Model
The affiliate whose tracking link was clicked most recently before a player registered receives 100% of the commission credit for that player. This is the industry default. If a player clicks L1 affiliate A's link on Day 1, then clicks L1 affiliate B's link on Day 3 and registers, affiliate B receives full attribution.
Hybrid
Combined CPA + RevShare
A bespoke commission structure offering both a reduced CPA on qualifying FTDs and a reduced RevShare rate on ongoing NGR. Provides the affiliate with immediate income and long-term upside. Hybrid terms must be individually negotiated and require Head of Affiliates approval before being offered.
Cookie Stuffing
Affiliate Fraud Technique
A fraudulent technique where an affiliate uses hidden scripts or iframes to drop tracking cookies on users' browsers without those users ever seeing or clicking an affiliate link. Results in false attribution of player registrations. Grounds for immediate termination and full clawback.
Philippines Regulatory Terms
CWT
Creditable Withholding Tax
BIR tax withheld by the operator from commission payments to individual Filipino affiliates. Rate: 10% (standard professional rate) or 8% (if affiliate has elected the 8% income tax option). The operator remits CWT to BIR on the affiliate's behalf. The affiliate receives BIR Form 2307 as proof of withholding to use as a tax credit in their annual ITR.
🇵🇭 BIR Requirement
EWT
Expanded Withholding Tax
BIR tax withheld from commission payments to corporate affiliate partners (domestic corporations and partnerships). Rate: 2% on commission amounts. Same remittance and Form 2307 requirements apply as for CWT.
🇵🇭 BIR Requirement
BIR Form 2307
Certificate of Creditable Tax Withheld
A BIR document issued by the operator to the affiliate each quarter confirming the amount of withholding tax deducted from their commissions. Affiliates use this form to claim a tax credit against their own income tax liability. Failure to issue Form 2307 is a BIR compliance violation with potential penalties.
🇵🇭 BIR — Quarterly
SEC Registration
Securities & Exchange Commission
Corporate affiliates (companies, partnerships) must be registered with the Philippine SEC. SEC registration documents are part of the KYC requirement for corporate affiliate onboarding. The SEC registration number must be included in the affiliate agreement for corporate partners.
🇵🇭 Corporate Requirement
PAGCOR Affiliate Rules
Marketing Compliance Framework
PAGCOR imposes specific restrictions on how licensed operators may market through affiliates. Key requirements: no targeting of minors, no advertising near schools or churches, responsible gambling messaging required in all affiliate content, affiliate channel documentation must be available for PAGCOR audit. Non-compliant affiliate activity is the operator's liability under the licence.
🇵🇭 PAGCOR Compliance
TIN
Tax Identification Number
A unique number issued by the BIR to all taxpayers in the Philippines. Both individual and corporate affiliates must provide a valid TIN before the first commission payment is processed. Commission payments cannot be made to affiliates without a TIN on file — this is an operator obligation, not merely a best practice.
🇵🇭 BIR Mandatory
