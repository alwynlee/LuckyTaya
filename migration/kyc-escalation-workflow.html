SLA: 4 hours

Actions at This Tier
  • Acknowledge the player's contact within 15 minutes via live chat or email — confirm receipt of their KYC query
  • Access the player account in the back-office system and check the KYC status flag and system rejection reason code
  • Identify the failure category using the rejection code lookup table (document issue, mismatch, incomplete)
  • For simple document issues: inform the player of exactly what is needed, provide the accepted document list, and request resubmission
  • Log the interaction in the CRM with the KYC ticket reference number, failure category, and player communication timestamp
  • If resubmission is requested: set a 48-hour player response timer in the ticketing system
  • If failure reason is unclear or not covered by the lookup table: create a KYC escalation ticket and pass to Tier 2 immediately

SLA: 24 hours
▾
Actions at This Tier
  • Review the full escalated KYC ticket including all player documents, system flags, and CS interaction log
  • Run manual identity verification checks against the submitted document set — cross-reference name, DOB, ID number
  • Conduct PEP and sanctions screening manually using the approved screening tool (e.g. World-Check, ComplyAdvantage) if system did not auto-screen

  • For identity mismatch: request a selfie with a handwritten date note alongside government-issued ID
  • For duplicate account flags: cross-check device fingerprint, IP, payment method, and document against existing accounts — document findings
  • Run the account through the internal risk scoring model and note the risk tier (low / medium / high)
  • Update the KYC case management system with full analyst notes, documents reviewed, and decision rationale
  • Notify the player of outcome or request for additional information within the SLA window

KYC Senior / Team Lead
Compliance Team · Senior review, EDD oversight, and authority to impose account restrictions
SLA: 48 hours
▾
Actions at This Tier
  • Take ownership of the full KYC case file including all Tier 2 analyst notes, documents, screening results, and risk scoring output
  • Conduct an independent secondary review of all screening results — do not rely solely on Tier 2 findings
  • For EDD cases: review source of funds documentation, employment verification, and any third-party source materials

  • Impose a temporary account deposit or withdrawal restriction pending outcome if the risk level warrants it
  • Prepare a written case summary and recommended outcome for Head of Compliance review if escalating to Tier 4
  • Ensure all actions and decisions are documented in the KYC case management system with timestamps

Head of Compliance / CCO
Executive authority · Final decision-maker · Regulatory liaison with PAGCOR and AMLC
SLA: 72 hours
▾
Actions at This Tier
  • Review the complete case file prepared by Tier 3 including all analyst notes, EDD documents, screening results, and STR draft if applicable
  • Make a final KYC acceptance or rejection decision with full documented rationale — this decision is binding

Regulatory Reporting Obligations
▼
AMLC / PAGCOR
Philippine Regulatory Reporting Requirements
AMLC — Anti-Money Laundering Council
  • STR (Suspicious Transaction Report): File within 5 business days of suspicion arising. Filed by CCO via AMLC's electronic reporting system. Do not inform the player (tipping-off prohibition).
  • CTR (Cash Transaction Report): File for single or linked transactions exceeding PHP 500,000 in any banking day. Applicable to deposits and withdrawals.
  • FSTR (Covered Transaction Freeze): Cooperate with AMLC freeze orders if a covered transaction is under investigation.
  • Record Retention: All KYC records must be retained for a minimum of 5 years after the end of the business relationship.
PAGCOR — Gaming Regulatory Obligations
  • Player Identity Verification: PAGCOR requires verified identity before any deposit or wager is accepted. No anonymous play permitted.
  • Incident Reporting: Notify PAGCOR Compliance Desk of any KYC case involving potential criminal activity or AMLC filing within 24 hours of the STR decision.
  • Excluded Persons: Cross-check all registrants against PAGCOR's Voluntary Exclusion Programme (VEP) list before activating any account.
  • Audit Readiness: Maintain a complete, timestamped KYC audit trail. PAGCOR auditors may request case files at any time with as little as 48 hours notice.

Internal Operations Standard

SLA & Response
Timers

Service level agreements for each escalation tier. All timers begin from the moment the case is received at that tier. SLA breaches must be logged and reported weekly to the Head of Compliance.

Tier SLA Targets
4h
Tier 1 · CS Agent
First contact response
CS must acknowledge the player and identify the failure category within 4 hours of first contact or system notification.
  • 15-minute acknowledgement SLA for live chat
  • 2-hour acknowledgement SLA for email
  • Player resubmission window: 48 hours
  • Escalation to Tier 2 if unresolved at 4h
24h
Tier 2 · KYC Analyst
Specialist review decision
Analyst must review, run screening, and reach a decision or escalate within 24 hours of ticket assignment.
  • Screening run within 2 hours of receipt
  • Player contacted for additional documents within 4 hours
  • Escalation to Tier 3 if decision not reached within 24h
  • PEP or watchlist hit: immediate escalation regardless of SLA clock
48h
Tier 3 · KYC Senior
EDD and complex case review
KYC Senior / Lead must complete EDD review, secondary screening, and reach a decision within 48 hours of case receipt.
  • EDD request to player sent within 4 hours of case receipt
  • Sanctions hit: escalate to CCO within 1 hour — no exception
  • STR draft prepared within 24h if suspicion confirmed
  • Escalation to Tier 4 if decision requires CCO authority
72h
Tier 4 · CCO
Final decision and regulatory action
CCO must review the complete case file, make a final decision, and initiate any regulatory filings within 72 hours.
  • AMLC STR filing: within 5 business days of suspicion arising (AMLA RA 9160)
  • PAGCOR incident report: within 24h of final rejection decision
  • CEO and Legal notified same day if AMLC filing is made
  • No further escalation — CCO decision is final
SLA Breach Consequences
Tier Breach Threshold Immediate Action Reporting Requirement Escalation
Tier 1 No CS contact within 4h Team Lead notified immediately. Case escalated to Tier 2 without waiting for CS resolution Logged in weekly CS SLA breach report to Head of CS Auto-escalate to Tier 2
Tier 2 No analyst decision within 24h KYC Senior takes over the case. Analyst must document reason for breach Logged in daily compliance case tracker. Reported to Head of Compliance weekly Auto-escalate to Tier 3
Tier 3 No Senior decision within 48h CCO notified directly. Account placed in full restriction (no deposits, no withdrawals) pending CCO review Reported to CCO same day. Included in monthly compliance board report CCO assumes case ownership
Tier 4 No CCO decision within 72h (non-AMLC) or 5 days (AMLC) Legal team notified. AMLC filing risk assessed. Account remains restricted Regulatory breach risk — must be documented in compliance register and reported to board Legal team engagement · Possible PAGCOR notification
⚠ Critical Non-Negotiable Timers
AMLC STR: Must be filed within 5 business days of the date the suspicion arose. Missing this deadline is a regulatory violation under RA 9160.
AMLC CTR: Filed within the same banking day for transactions ≥ PHP 500,000.
Sanctions Hit: Account freeze must occur within 1 hour of confirmed match. No exceptions.
Minor Detected: Account suspension is immediate — zero delay permitted. Funds refunded within 24h.
PAGCOR VEP: Registration block must be applied in real time at the point of KYC check. No grace period.
Tipping-Off: Once an STR is filed or in preparation, the player must not be informed under any circumstances. This applies to all tiers.

KYC Glossary

Key terms, acronyms, and regulatory bodies referenced in the KYC escalation workflow. All staff involved in KYC processing are expected to be familiar with these definitions.

Regulatory Bodies
AMLC
Anti-Money Laundering Council
The primary government agency in the Philippines responsible for implementing the Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA, RA 9160 as amended). Receives and investigates STRs and CTRs filed by covered entities including iGaming operators.
🇵🇭 PH Regulatory Body
PAGCOR
Philippine Amusement and Gaming Corporation
The government-owned and controlled corporation that regulates and licenses all gaming operations in the Philippines. Issues iGaming licences and enforces responsible gambling and KYC requirements on licensed operators.
🇵🇭 PH Regulatory Body
BSP
Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas
The central bank of the Philippines. Regulates payment service providers (PSPs) including GCash and Maya. Relevant to KYC when payment method flags arise or when e-money accounts are involved in suspicious transactions.
🇵🇭 PH Regulatory Body
NBI
National Bureau of Investigation
Philippine law enforcement body. Relevant when forged documents or confirmed fraud is identified — a referral to NBI may be required in cases involving criminal activity.
🇵🇭 PH Law Enforcement
NPC
National Privacy Commission
Regulates the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173). KYC document storage, player data handling, and data breach notification obligations fall under NPC jurisdiction. All KYC records must be stored securely and access-controlled.
🇵🇭 PH Data Regulator
KYC & AML Terms
KYC
Know Your Customer
The process of verifying the identity of a customer before or during the time they start doing business with the company. Includes identity verification, document checks, and risk assessment.
AML
Anti-Money Laundering
The set of laws, regulations, and procedures intended to prevent criminals from disguising illegally obtained funds as legitimate income. AML compliance is a core requirement of the PAGCOR licence.
EDD
Enhanced Due Diligence
A deeper level of customer due diligence applied to higher-risk customers including PEPs, high-volume depositors, or players flagged by the risk scoring model. Requires source of funds documentation and more frequent review.
CDD
Customer Due Diligence
Standard verification process applied to all new players: identity verification, document check, and basic risk assessment. EDD is the elevated version applied to higher-risk cases.
STR
Suspicious Transaction Report
A report filed with the AMLC when a transaction is deemed suspicious — i.e. there are reasonable grounds to believe funds are proceeds of an unlawful activity. Must be filed within 5 business days of suspicion arising. Tipping-off is prohibited.
🇵🇭 AMLC Filing
CTR
Cash Transaction Report
A report filed with AMLC for single or linked cash transactions exceeding PHP 500,000 in any banking day. Applies to player deposits and withdrawals at or above this threshold. Filed on the same banking day.
🇵🇭 AMLC Filing
PEP
Politically Exposed Person
An individual who holds or has held a prominent public position (e.g. government official, senior military officer, senior executive of a state-owned enterprise). PEPs and their close associates and family members require enhanced due diligence and mandatory CCO approval before account activation.
VEP
Voluntary Exclusion Programme
PAGCOR's programme allowing players to self-exclude from all licensed gaming establishments in the Philippines. Players on the VEP list must be blocked from registering or accessing their account. No exceptions are permitted.
🇵🇭 PAGCOR Programme
Tipping-Off
Prohibited Disclosure
The act of informing a customer or any other person that an STR has been filed or is being prepared against them. This is a criminal offence under Philippine law. Once an STR decision is being considered, all player communication must be paused and reviewed by the CCO before sending.
AMLA
Anti-Money Laundering Act (RA 9160)
The primary legislation governing AML obligations in the Philippines. As amended by RA 9194, RA 10167, RA 10365, and RA 10927 (which extended AMLA coverage to casinos including online gaming). All iGaming operators must comply.
🇵🇭 PH Legislation
Liveness Check
Biometric Identity Verification
A real-time verification step where the player takes a selfie or video to prove they are a live person (not a photo or mask) and that their face matches the submitted government ID. Failure of liveness check escalates to Tier 2 analyst review.
Risk Scoring
Internal Risk Assessment Model
A scoring system applied to each player during KYC review. Factors include: country of origin, source of funds, PEP status, deposit volume, and transaction behaviour. Output is a risk tier (low / medium / high) that determines the level of ongoing monitoring required.
Accepted Philippine KYC Documents

Passport · Driver's Licence (LTO) · SSS Card / UMID · PhilHealth ID · Postal ID · PRC ID · Voter's ID · Philippine National ID (PhilSys)
