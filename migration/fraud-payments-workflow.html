Detection Layer — Triggers
Automated Rules Engine Flags
  • Multiple accounts sharing the same device fingerprint, IP address, or browser profile
  • Deposit from a payment method already registered on another account
  • Bonus claim velocity: same promo claimed within 48h from devices on the same network
  • Withdrawal request within 30 minutes of initial deposit with minimal or no play
  • Transaction velocity: 5+ deposits or withdrawals within a 2-hour window
  • Bet pattern anomaly: near-zero house edge play (e.g. alternating red/black, backing both sides)
  • OTP verification from an unrecognised device + immediate withdrawal attempt = ATO indicator
  • Daily transaction aggregate reaching 80% of the ₱500k AMLC CTR threshold — pre-flag alert
  • Login from a new country or VPN when the player has a consistent PH login history
  • Player uses 3+ different payment methods within 7 days — potential carding signal
Manual Flagging Sources
  • CS Agent observes unusual player behaviour or receives a complaint from another player about suspected collusion
  • VIP Account Manager notices third-party deposit — someone other than the account holder is topping up the account
  • Payments team flags a chargeback or e-wallet dispute filed against a recent deposit
  • KYC Analyst identifies document or identity patterns matching a previously rejected or banned account
  • Sportsbook Trading flags an abnormal betting pattern on a specific match or event — potential match fixing signal
  • External intelligence: industry fraud network alert or PAGCOR notification about a known fraudster operating in the PH market
  • Player self-reports that their account has been accessed without their knowledge (ATO victim report)
// PH FRAUD CONTEXT
Common Philippines-specific fraud patterns: GCash account farming using pre-registered SIMs (leveraging the PH SIM registration gaps), OTP interception via social engineering on GCash helpdesk impersonation, coordinated bonus abuse rings operating through Telegram groups, and structured deposits just below ₱500k to avoid AMLC CTR filing.
Investigation Workflow — 3 Tiers
T1
Fraud Analyst — Initial Triage
First responder · Rules engine alerts · Preliminary evidence gathering
SLA: 1 HOUR
▾
Triage Actions
  • Receive alert from the automated rules engine or manual flag — acknowledge within 15 minutes and assign a case reference number
  • Pull the player's full account profile: registration date, KYC status, deposit history, withdrawal history, bonus claims, login history, device and IP log
  • Identify the primary fraud typology from the flag and cross-reference against the fraud typology library
  • Apply an immediate soft account restriction if the fraud risk is rated High or Critical: suspend deposits and withdrawals pending investigation. Do not suspend the account entirely at this stage — preserve the player's ability to contact CS
  • Document all findings, data points, and screenshots in the case management system with timestamps
  • Run device fingerprint, IP, and payment method cross-reference to identify any linked accounts
  • If linked accounts are found: flag all linked accounts for investigation and expand the case scope immediately
  • Determine if the case requires escalation to Tier 2 within 1 hour or can be resolved at Tier 1

⛔

T2
Senior Fraud Analyst — Deep Investigation
Evidence compilation · Payment tracing · AMLC coordination · Account suspension authority
SLA: 24 HOURS
▾
Investigation Actions
  • Take ownership of the full case file from Tier 1. Conduct an independent review — do not rely solely on T1 findings
  • Trace the full deposit and withdrawal chain: map all payment methods used, source wallets, destination accounts, and timeline
  • For ATO cases: cross-reference login timestamps with the player's self-reported normal activity. Check for concurrent sessions or impossible geography (two logins from distant locations within minutes)
  • For payment fraud: contact the PSP (GCash Business, Maya Business, UnionBank) via the dedicated fraud reporting channel to confirm whether the payment instrument is flagged or disputed
  • For multi-account rings: map the full network of linked accounts. Identify the primary account and any secondary accounts created to exploit bonuses or launder funds
  • For match fixing signals: coordinate with the Sportsbook Trading team to obtain the bet data and market context. Cross-reference with any industry intelligence feeds
  • Compile a structured evidence package: screenshots, transaction logs, device data, payment traces, and a written investigation summary
  • Make a preliminary recommendation: Account suspension, fund freeze, AMLC referral, or escalation to Head of Fraud

Chargeback Response Process
  • PSP notifies platform of a chargeback or dispute filing via the PSP merchant dashboard or email alert. Log immediately with the chargeback reference number
  • Identify the deposit transaction, player account, and the stated reason for the dispute (unauthorised transaction, service not received, fraudulent merchant)
  • Compile the evidence package within 48 hours: transaction timestamp, PSP transaction ID, player login records at time of deposit, game play records showing funds were used, KYC documents, and any CS communication history with the player
  • Payments Specialist reviews and determines if the chargeback is: (a) Fraudulent chargeback by the player — contest with evidence, (b) Legitimate ATO — player did not make the deposit — accept chargeback and initiate fraud investigation, (c) PSP error — escalate to PSP directly
  • For contested chargebacks: submit evidence to the PSP chargeback portal within the 5-business-day window. Any submission after the deadline is automatically lost
  • Suspend the player account pending resolution if the chargeback indicates potential fraud
  • If chargeback rate for a specific PSP exceeds 1% in any month: escalate to Head of Fraud & Payments for PSP relationship review
// BSP MEDIATION
If a GCash or Maya dispute cannot be resolved directly with the PSP, the player may escalate to the BSP Consumer Assistance Mechanism (CAM). The operator must maintain complete transaction records for a minimum of 5 years to defend BSP-mediated disputes. BSP resolution timelines vary (30–90 days). Payments team must track all active BSP dispute references.

Fraud & Payments
Glossary

Key terms, acronyms, Philippines-specific payment infrastructure, and regulatory definitions for the Fraud and Payments operations workflow.

Fraud Terms
ATO
Account Takeover
Unauthorized access to a legitimate player account by a third party, typically via stolen credentials, phishing, SIM swap, or brute force. ATOs are typically followed immediately by withdrawal attempts. Account must be frozen within 15 minutes of suspicion.
Device Fingerprint
Browser / Device Identifier
A unique identifier created from a combination of browser settings, screen resolution, installed fonts, plugins, timezone, and hardware attributes. Used to link multiple accounts operating from the same device, even if different player credentials are used.
Velocity Check
Transaction Frequency Rule
An automated rule that flags accounts making an unusually high number of transactions within a defined time window. For example: 5+ deposits within 2 hours, or 3+ bonus claims within 48 hours. High velocity on deposits or withdrawals is a primary fraud indicator.
Structuring
Transaction Structuring / Smurfing
The deliberate splitting of transactions to stay below the AMLC CTR threshold of ₱500,000. For example, making multiple deposits of ₱490,000–₱499,000 on consecutive days. Structuring is itself a criminal offence under AMLA RA 9160 regardless of the source of funds.
🇵🇭 AMLC — AMLA RA 9160
Bonus Abuse
Promotional Exploitation
Systematic exploitation of welcome bonuses, cashback offers, or free bets through multiple accounts (bonus multi-accounting), near-zero-risk play strategies to clear wagering requirements, or coordinated rings of players sharing bonuses across accounts.
Collusion
Coordinated Player Fraud
Two or more players coordinating to gain an unfair advantage, most commonly in poker (sharing hole card information) or PvP specialty games (one player deliberately losing to another to transfer funds). Match fixing in sportsbook falls under a related but distinct category involving sporting event manipulation.
SIM Swap
SIM Hijacking
A fraud method where an attacker convinces a mobile network operator to transfer a victim's phone number to a SIM card controlled by the attacker, thereby intercepting OTP messages. Particularly relevant in the Philippines where GCash and Maya use OTP-based authentication. Indicators include: sudden OTP failures reported by the legitimate user, or a player account accessing GCash from a new device immediately after a period of inactivity.
🇵🇭 PH Context — GCash / Maya
Tipping-Off
Prohibited Disclosure
The act of informing a subject that they are under investigation for money laundering or that an STR has been filed. A criminal offence under AMLA. Once an AMLC referral is in progress, all player-facing communication must be cleared by the CCO. CS and VIP teams must not reference the investigation to the player under any circumstances.
🇵🇭 AMLA RA 9160
Payments & PSP Terms
PSP
Payment Service Provider
A third-party provider that processes payment transactions between the operator and the player. In the Philippines context, the primary PSPs are GCash (Mynt/Globe), Maya (PLDT Voyager), UnionBank, and BDO. Each PSP has its own API, settlement timeline, dispute process, and fraud reporting channel.
Chargeback
Payment Reversal Dispute
A reversal of a payment initiated by the customer through their PSP or bank. In iGaming, chargebacks are filed by players who claim the deposit was unauthorised (ATO) or who deliberately attempt to reclaim funds after losing (friendly fraud). The operator has a fixed window (typically 5 business days) to submit evidence contesting the chargeback.
Reconciliation
Transaction Ledger Matching
The daily process of matching the operator's internal transaction ledger against the PSP settlement report to identify discrepancies. Discrepancies can arise from timing differences, failed transactions that were partially processed, or PSP errors. All discrepancies must be identified within 24 hours and resolved within 72 hours.
InstaPay
BSP Real-Time Payment Rail
A BSP-regulated interbank real-time fund transfer service available 24/7. Maximum per-transaction amount is ₱50,000. Settlement is near-instant. The most common rail for standard player withdrawals via bank transfer in the Philippines.
🇵🇭 BSP — Real-Time
PESONet
BSP Batch Payment Rail
A BSP-regulated batch electronic fund transfer system. Transactions submitted before the batch cut-off (typically 12pm–2pm) are settled on the same business day. Transactions after the cut-off are settled the next business day. No maximum per-transaction limit, making it suitable for large Platinum and Elite withdrawals.
🇵🇭 BSP — Batch
Name-Match Check
Withdrawal Destination Verification
A verification step that compares the name on the player's KYC-approved identity document against the registered name on the GCash account, Maya account, or bank account to which a withdrawal is being sent. Mismatches must be investigated before disbursement — third-party withdrawals are a key money laundering red flag.
🇵🇭 AMLC Requirement
CTR
Cash Transaction Report
Filed with AMLC when a player's single or linked transactions in a single banking day reach or exceed ₱500,000. Filed on the same banking day. The Payments team is responsible for alerting Compliance when a player's daily total approaches this threshold — the Fraud & Payments team tracks this in real time via the transaction monitoring dashboard.
🇵🇭 AMLC — Same Banking Day
BSP CAM
Consumer Assistance Mechanism
The Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas formal dispute resolution process for unresolved payment complaints. Players can escalate to BSP CAM if GCash or Maya does not resolve their dispute within the PSP's own internal timeline. The operator must maintain full transaction records and be prepared to respond to BSP inquiries, which can carry regulatory penalties for non-compliance.
🇵🇭 BSP — Regulatory Escalation
Regulatory Reference
AMLA RA 9160
Anti-Money Laundering Act (as amended)
The primary Philippines legislation governing AML obligations. Extended to cover online casinos and iGaming operators by RA 10927 (2017). Key obligations: KYC, CTR filing, STR filing, record retention (5 years), and prohibition on tipping-off. CCO bears personal liability for compliance failures.
🇵🇭 Primary AML Legislation
NBI
National Bureau of Investigation
The Philippine federal law enforcement agency with jurisdiction over cybercrime, financial fraud, and organised crime. Relevant for iGaming operators when fraud cases involve criminal activity warranting a formal complaint — particularly for organised fraud rings, large-scale ATO attacks, or match-fixing investigations with criminal elements.
🇵🇭 Law Enforcement
PAGCOR
Philippine Amusement and Gaming Corporation
Primary gaming regulator and licencing authority. Operators must notify PAGCOR of significant fraud incidents, match-fixing cases, and any AMLC filings relating to gaming activity. PAGCOR may also issue operator-level guidance on emerging fraud typologies in the Philippine iGaming market.
🇵🇭 Gaming Regulator
BSP
Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas
Central bank of the Philippines. Regulates all payment service providers including GCash and Maya. Relevant to the Fraud & Payments team when: PSP disputes escalate to BSP CAM, e-money regulatory obligations apply, or when a payment fraud case involves the banking system.
🇵🇭 Central Bank
